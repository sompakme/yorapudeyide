Chris Parnell Biography

Chris Parnell (Thomas Christopher Parnell ) is an American actor and comedian. He was born and raised in Memphis, Tennessee, U.S. Parnell is best recognized for being a cast member on Saturday Night Live from 1998 to 2006, where he played the role of Dr. Leo Spaceman on 30 Rock.

In animation, he voices Cyril Figgis on the FX series Archer, Jerry Smith on Adult Swim’s Rick and Morty, and the narrator on the PBS Kids series WordGirl. Moreover, he voices “The Progressive Box” in a series of advertisements by the Progressive Corporation.

Parnell Education

Parnell attended the Southern Baptist Educational Center and went on to graduate from Germantown High School. At age 17, he resolved to pursue a career in acting and as a result, he enrolled at the University of North Carolina School of the Arts. Here, Chris earned his BFA in Drama.

After college, Parnell relocated to Houston, Texas to undertake an apprentice actor program for a season at the Alley Theatre, unfortunately, he wasn’t requested to join the company back again.

Parnell became momentarily disenchanted with acting, and returned to his hometown, securing a job as a high school teacher for a year. Chris then moved to Los Angeles and auditioned for the Groundlings in 1992.

Chris Parnell SNL

Parnell has joined the  Saturday Night Live cast as a featured player, making his debut on September 26, 1998. The following season, he was promoted to repertory player. In the summer of 2001, due to budget cuts, Lorne Michaels was forced to dismiss two cast members; he chose to lay off Parnell and Jerry Minor, but Parnell was rehired in the middle of the following season.

Parnell appeared in many sketches, and commercial parodies and performed impressions of various celebrities while on SNL. Some of his notable sketches are “Lazy Sunday”, a rap video he shot with Andy Samberg, and “More Cowbell”. He has performed raps about hosts Jennifer Garner, Britney Spears, Kirsten Dunst, and Ashton Kutcher.

On September 22, 2006, it was announced that three cast members had been laid off: Parnell; Horatio Sanz; and Finesse Mitchell. This made him the only SNL performer to have been released twice by Lorne Michaels.

Chris had been with SNL for eight seasons. He has since made uncredited cameo appearances on the show, including parodying newscasters Tom Brokaw, Jim Lehrer, and Bob Schieffer.

Parnell and his former SNL castmate Horatio Sanz starred together in Big Lake, a 2010 sitcom on Comedy Central from executive producers Will Ferrell and Adam McKay.

Chris Parnell Rick And Morty

Chris Parnell Archer

Parnell provides the voice for Cyril Figgis on Archer, a half-hour animated comedy series created by Adam Reed for the FX network. The first season premiered on Thursday, September 17, 2009. Frequent Adam Reed collaborator Matt Thompson is the Executive Producer.

Chris Parnell Anchorman
