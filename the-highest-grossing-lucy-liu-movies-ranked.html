The Highest-Grossing Lucy Liu Movies, Ranked

Lucy Liu, the powerhouse of talent, has graced the silver screen with her captivating performances and undeniable charisma. Born on December 2, 1968, in Queens, New York, Liu has carved her path in the entertainment industry, leaving an indelible mark. In this electrifying piece, we delve into the realm of box office success to unveil the top 10 highest-grossing movies in Lucy Liu's filmography. With a vast array of action-packed blockbusters and heartfelt comedies, Liu's versatility shines through. Join us as we rank these cinematic triumphs, exploring the movies that have catapulted Liu into the realm of Hollywood legends.

Kill Bill: Volume 2 - 2004 ($153.5 Million)

Via Flickr/Catchplay

Lucy graced the screen as the fierce O-Ren Ishi in Kill Bill: Volume 2. Directed by Quentin Tarantino. This action-packed extravaganza took us on a blood-soaked journey through Liu's portrayal of the deadly assassin. With a worldwide gross of $153.5 million, this film solidified Liu's status as a force to be reckoned with in the industry. Her electrifying presence created a cinematic masterpiece that left audiences breathless and begging for more.

Payback - 1999 ($161.6 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

It is a wild ride in the gritty world of crime with Payback, where Lucy Liu shines as the captivating Pearl. This action thriller, directed by Brian Helgeland, showcases Liu's versatility and magnetic screen presence. As Pearl, Liu effortlessly steals every scene she's in, leaving us craving for more of her dynamic company. Payback’s thrilling adventure filled with unexpected twists and turns propelled it to a worldwide gross of $161.6 million.

Kill Bill: Volume 1 - 2003 ($176.4 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

The first installment of the iconic Kill Bill series, where Lucy Liu delivers an unforgettable performance as the vengeful O-Ren Ishi is a must-watch. Directed by Quentin Tarantino, Kill Bill: Volume 1 combines explosive action, stylish visuals, and Liu's commanding presence to create a cinematic experience like no other. Boasting a worldwide box office gross of $176.4 million, this film became a cultural phenomenon, solidifying Liu's status as an action superstar.

Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle - 2003 ($227.1 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Lucy Liu reprised her role as Alex in Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle which was directed by McG. This action-comedy extravaganza took the audience on a wild ride filled with explosive stunts, sizzling chemistry, and Liu's undeniable charm. Its worldwide box office gross of $227.1 million catapulted Liu and her fellow Angels to new heights of stardom and her portrayal of Alex showcases her impeccable comedic timing and magnetic screen presence.

Charlie's Angels - 2000 ($257.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the world of espionage and female empowerment of Charlie's Angels, Lucy Liu shines as the fiercely independent Alex. Directed by McG, this action-packed film combines heart-pounding thrills, hilarious banter, and Liu's undeniable charisma. Charlie's Angels became a cultural phenomenon, inspiring audiences with its empowering message and showcasing Liu's ability to kick some serious butt with a worldwide box office gross of $257.7 million.

Jerry Maguire - 1996 ($273.5 Million)

Via Flickr/WECAN

Lucy Liu embarks on a heartfelt journey of love and self-discovery in the critically acclaimed film Jerry Maguire. Directed by Cameron Crowe, this romantic comedy-drama follows the tumultuous relationship between Jerry Maguire and his former girlfriend. With a worldwide gross of $273.5 million, the film struck a chord with audiences, thanks in part to Liu's compelling portrayal of the enigmatic former flame.

Chicago - 2002 ($306.7 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Liu dazzles as the seductive Go-to-Hell-Kitty in Chicago, which was directed by Rob Marshall. This Academy Award-winning film combines stunning choreography, unforgettable songs, and Liu's mesmerizing performance to hit a worldwide box office gross of $306.7 million. Liu's portrayal of Go-to-Hell-Kitty is both sultry and magnetic, adding an extra layer of intrigue to this captivating tale of fame, fortune, and the pursuit of dreams.

Kung Fu Panda 3 - 2016 ($521.1 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Lucy lends her voice to the lovable Viper in Kung Fu Panda 3, directed by Jennifer Yuh Nelson and Alessandro Carloni, this animated adventure takes us on a hilarious and action-packed journey alongside Po and the Furious Five. Kung Fu Panda 3 became an instant favorite among audiences of all ages and its worldwide box office gross of $521.1 million is a testament to that. Liu's voice acting brings Viper to life with charm and wit, making her an integral part of Po's epic martial arts journey.

Kung Fu Panda - 2008 ($631.9 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Kung Fu Panda, in which Lucy Liu lends her voice to the delightful character Viper was directed by Mark Osborne and John Stevenson. This animated gem became an instant classic, enchanting audiences with its heartwarming story and dazzling animation. With a worldwide box office gross of $631.9 million, Kung Fu Panda soared to incredible heights, thanks partly to Liu's voice acting which adds a perfect blend of humor and warmth to this beloved animated adventure.

Kung Fu Panda 2 - 2011 ($664.8 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the enchanting world of Po and his fellow warriors in Kung Fu Panda 2, Lucy Liu once again lends her voice to the lovable Viper as directed by Jennifer Yuh Nelson. This animated sequel takes us on an epic journey filled with laughter, heart, and thrilling action. The movie captured the hearts of audiences worldwide, showcasing Liu's incredible voice-acting talents and hauling a worldwide gross of $664.8 million. Her portrayal of Viper brings delightful energy and charm to the character, making her an integral part of the Kung Fu Panda franchise.
