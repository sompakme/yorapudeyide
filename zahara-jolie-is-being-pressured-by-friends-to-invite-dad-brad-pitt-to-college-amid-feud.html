Zahara Jolie Is Being Pressured By Friends To Invite Dad Brad Pitt To College Amid Feud

Highlights

  • Brad Pitt's strained relationship with his children, particularly Zahara, has been highlighted through recent events like Zahara dropping his last name after starting college.
  • Zahara's college friends are reportedly excited at the prospect of meeting Brad Pitt, and they want him to visit her on campus.
  • However, it's unclear if Zahara herself wants Brad around, as she recently distanced herself from him by using only her mother's last name and a post from their son Pax criticized Brad for allegedly making their lives a "constant hell." The custody battle between Brad and Angelina is ongoing.

Brad Pitt’s tense relationship with his children has been revealed in several ways recently, but he may soon get an invitation to his daughter Zahara’s college, if her friends have any say.

Zahara recently made headlines for dropping her father’s last name after beginning college. However, a new report says that her peers are excited at the prospect of meeting Zahara’s famous father, Brad Pitt.

Brad’s Daughter Zahara May Not Want Him On Campus

Zahara is currently attending Spelman College in Atlanta, Georgia, a historically Black university. For now, it remains unclear if Brad will go to visit his daughter on campus.

But even if facing pressure from her peers, Zahara may not be interested in having Brad around, especially if her recent public appearance was any indication. Earlier this month, footage of Zahara joining the sorority Alpha Kappa Alpha went viral after she introduced herself as “Zahara Jolie.”

All of Brangelina’s kids had the surname “Jolie-Pitt” at birth, but Zahara’s sorority event indicates she’s only using her mom’s last name, potentially in an attempt to distance herself from Brad amid their reportedly strained relationship.

Brad’s Son Pax Criticized Him In A Secret Instagram Post

Also, this month, a past post made to a secret Instagram account by Brad and Angelina’s son Pax revealed his difficult relationship with his father. The post, which was made on Father’s Day in 2020 but only publicly revealed recently, slams Brad for the hurt he allegedly inflicted on the entire family.

“You have made the lives of those closest to me a constant hell,” Pax wrote in the post. “You may tell yourself and the world whatever you want. But the truth will come to light someday.”
