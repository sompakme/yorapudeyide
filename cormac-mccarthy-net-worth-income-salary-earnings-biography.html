Cormac Mccarthy Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

‘Cormac McCarthy’ (born Charles McCarthy on July 20, 1933) is an American author, dramatist, and screenwriter who lives in New York City with his family. He has authored eleven books, which cover a wide range of genres, including Southern Gothic, Western, and post-apocalyptic fiction. For his novel The Road, he was awarded the Pulitzer Prize as well as the James Tait Black Memorial Prize for Fiction (2006). His book No Country for Old Men, published in 2005, was turned into a film of the same name, released in 2007, which won four Academy Awards, including Best Picture. 

In 1992, he received the National Book Award in the United States as well as the National Book Critics Circle Award for his novel, All the Pretty Horses. A number of novels, including all of the Pretty Horses, The Road, and Child of God, have been made into films. Among Time magazine’s list of the 100 greatest English-language novels written between 1923 and 2005 was Blood Meridian (1985), which also came in second place in a vote conducted by The New York Times in 2006 to choose the finest American fiction produced in the previous 25 years. 

Along with Don DeLillo, Thomas Pynchon, and Philip Roth, literary critic Harold Bloom identified him as one of the four main American writers of his generation, and hailed Blood Meridian as “the finest single work since Faulkner’s As I Lay Dying.” The Road was rated #1 on The Times’s list of the 100 greatest fiction and non-fiction books published in the previous ten years, published in 2010. McCarthy’s name has being discussed more and more as a possible nominee for the Nobel Prize in Literature.
