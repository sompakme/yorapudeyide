Isabelle Fuhrman Biography

Isabelle Fuhrman is an American actress who was born and brought up in Washington, D.C., United States. She is famously known for her roles as Clove in the 2012 dystopian adventure film The Hunger Games and as Esther in the 2009 psychological horror film Orphan. She is also known for her television series such as Pleading Guilty(2010), Adventure Time(2013), and Children of the Corn(2009)

Isabelle Actress

Isabelle started her acting career at the age of seven.  A casting director from Cartoon Network spotted her waiting for her older sister Madeline and cast her for one of the shows, Cartoon Fridays. She made her big-screen debut just a few years later in the 2007 drama Hounddog. Isabelle was picked to star in the movie Orphan alongside Vera Farmiga and Peter Sarsgaard in 2007 and she was cast after a nationwide search of young actresses to play the lead in the Warner Bros. collaboration between Leonardo DiCaprio’s Appian Way and Joel Silver’s Dark Castle Entertainment.

Isabelle played Angie Vanderveer in 2011 in the dark comedy Salvation Boulevard with an ensemble cast that included Pierce Brosnan and Marisa Tomei, and which premiered at the Sundance Film Festival. She voiced genetically enhanced assassin Victoria in Hitman: Absolution in 2012. The same year she portrayed Clove, a career tribute who tries to kill the main character, Katniss, in the film The Hunger Games.

Isabelle appeared as Max in Kevin Connolly’s next movie, Dear Eleanor on 24 May 2013 a film scheduled for release in 2015. That following year, she appeared in the film Cell, an adaptation by Stephen King. In 2015, she appeared in a “major recurring role” on the Showtime drama series Masters of Sex, playing Tessa, the daughter of Virginia Johnson (Lizzy Caplan).
