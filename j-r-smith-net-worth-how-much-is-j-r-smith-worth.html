NBA Career

J.R. Smith’s NBA career has been marked by his impressive performances and contributions to multiple teams. He was selected by the Charlotte Hornets in the 2004 NBA Draft, where he began his professional journey. Over the years, Smith showcased his skills and versatility by playing for various teams, including the Denver Nuggets, New York Knicks, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Los Angeles Lakers.

Smith’s time with the Cavaliers and Lakers was particularly noteworthy, as he played a vital role in helping both teams secure NBA championships. In 2016, he contributed significantly to the Cavaliers’ historic championship run, where they overcame a 3-1 deficit against the Golden State Warriors. Then, in 2020, Smith joined the Lakers and played a key role in their championship-winning season.

In addition to his championship success, J.R. Smith has received recognition for his individual achievements. In 2013, he was honored as the NBA Sixth Man of the Year, a testament to his invaluable contributions off the bench.
