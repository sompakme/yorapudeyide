Janel Parrish Young Cosette

Parrish started her career in acting when she cast as Young Cosette in the National Touring Company of Les Misérables and later, she portrayed the role for two months in Broadway production at the Imperial Theatre. Parrish landed her first television role in NBC’s action series Baywatch, appearing in two episodes as Hina, a young girl who Mitch Buchannon (David Hasselhoff) saves from drowning in 1999. Parrish was featured in Disney’s live-action television film based on the Pinocchio story, Geppetto, portraying the role of Natalie in 2002.

Janel Parrish Pretty Little Liars

Her role was her first in a motion picture, as well as her first starring role. Shortly before the release of Bratz in may 2007, she was signed into the  William Morris Agency. She then went on the recurring role in the NBC supernatural drama series Heroes. As a role, she played Mona Vanderwaal for the duration of the Freeform teen drama series Pretty Little Liars, based on the book series by Sara Shepard. Parrish was promoted to a series regular for her performance as Mona, for the third season and stayed a regular for the remainder of the series in March 2012.  she also won the  Choice TV – Villain at the Teen Choice Awards four times from five nominations. Parrish returned to her stage roots in a production of the musical Spring Awakening In February 2012. She portrayed herself as Anna, while also understudying the lead female role of Wendla Bergmann.

She had a supporting role in the romantic comedy-drama film Celeste and Jesse Forever the same year,  and she made a guest appearance in Lifetime’s fantasy legal drama series Drop Dead Diva as cheerleader Chelsea Putnam.  Parrish also co-starred as Margot Covey in the film adaptation of Jenny Han’s young adult romance novel To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before, with Lana Condor and John Corbett, and appeared alongside Angela Trimbur and Fairuza Balk in the home-invasion thriller Hell Is Where the Home Is. Parrish will co-star with Mickey Rourke in the upcoming boxing drama Tiger. She is set to reprise her role as Mona Vanderwaal in the Pretty Little Liars spin-off/sequel drama series, Pretty Little Liars: The Perfectionists.
